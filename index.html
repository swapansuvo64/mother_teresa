Biography

Mother Teresa, born AnjezÃ« Gonxhe Bojaxhiu on August 26, 1910, in Skopje (now in North Macedonia), was a Roman Catholic nun and missionary. She devoted her life to serving the poor, the sick, and the dying, especially in Kolkata, India. She founded the Missionaries of Charity in 1950, and for over 45 years, she cared for the most destitute, establishing homes for those suffering from leprosy, AIDS, and other illnesses.

She won numerous awards for her humanitarian work, including the Nobel Peace Prize in 1979. Mother Teresa passed away on September 5, 1997, but her legacy of compassion and service continues to inspire people worldwide.

Achievements

  • Nobel Peace Prize (1979)
  • Bharat Ratna (India's highest civilian award, 1980)
  • Order of Merit from Queen Elizabeth II (1983)
  • Pope John XXIII Peace Prize (1971)
  • Presidential Medal of Freedom (1985)

Legacy

Mother Teresa's work continues through the Missionaries of Charity, which operates in over 130 countries, providing care for the poorest of the poor. Her philosophy of selfless service has inspired countless volunteers and organizations to follow in her footsteps. In 2016, she was canonized as Saint Teresa of Calcutta by the Catholic Church.

Famous Quotes

"Not all of us can do great things. But we can do small things with great love."
"If you can't feed a hundred people, then feed just one."
"I alone cannot change the world, but I can cast a stone across the waters to create many ripples."
